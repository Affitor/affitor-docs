## Pricing & Fees

### How much does Affitor cost?

Affitor charges a **3.5% platform fee** on affiliate-generated revenue. That's it.

- No setup fees
- No monthly subscriptions
- No retainers
- No hidden charges

### What about the $10K revenue waiver?

For qualified partners, we offer a **limited-time promotion** where platform fees are waived until affiliate-generated revenue exceeds $10,000. During this period, you only pay partner commissions—Affitor charges nothing.

### How are fees calculated?

Fees are calculated on **actual received revenue** after refunds, chargebacks, and failed transactions. Taxes and payment processing fees (like Stripe's 2.9%) are borne by you as the advertiser, not deducted from affiliate or platform economics.

**Example: $30/month SaaS subscription**

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Gross revenue | $30.00 |
| Stripe fee (2.9% + $0.30) | -$1.17 |
| Affiliate commission (30%) | -$9.00 |
| Affitor platform fee (3.5%) | -$1.05 |
| **You keep** | **$18.78** |

**Your effective CAC:** $10.05 (affiliate + Affitor)
**Your net margin:** 62.6% of gross revenue

For comparison, if you paid $50+ per lead on Google Ads with uncertain conversion—this is a predictable, performance-based cost only when real revenue comes in.

### When do I pay?

Platform fees are invoiced monthly based on tracked conversions after the standard refund window (typically 30 days).

## Payouts & Commissions

### Who pays the affiliates?

Affitor handles partner payouts on your behalf. Here's how it works:

1. **Sale happens** — Customer purchases via affiliate link
2. **Invoice sent** — You pay commission + platform fee
   - Split Pay: Automatic per transaction (via Stripe Connect)
   - Bill Flow: Weekly summary invoice
3. **Funds received** — Money moves to Affitor's holding balance
4. **Hold period clears** — 7-21 days (configurable)
5. **Bi-weekly payout** — Affitor pays partner

This removes operational burden from your team while ensuring partners get paid reliably.

### What payout methods are available for partners?

Partners can receive payouts via:

- **Bank transfer** (global)
- **Wise** (1-2 business days)
- **Stripe** (where available)

### How often are partners paid?

**Bi-weekly** (every 2 weeks). Partners receive all cleared commissions in their Withdrawable balance on a fixed cycle.

Minimum payout threshold: **$5**

### What happens if there's a refund?

Depends on your integration method:

**Split Pay:**
- Refund detected automatically
- Funds return to your Affitor balance immediately
- Partner commission reversed automatically

**Bill Flow:**
- Refund reconciled at next payment cycle
- Adjustment applied to upcoming payout

In both cases:
- Affitor refunds its 3.5% platform fee
- Partner commission clawed back from their balance
- If partner has positive balance, deducted from next payout

## Brand Protection

### How do you prevent fraud?

We're committed to zero spam, zero fraud, and zero brand-damaging behavior. Our platform includes:

- Partner vetting during recruitment
- Activity monitoring for suspicious patterns
- Clear Partner Terms enforcement
- Immediate action on violations

### Can I set rules for partner promotion?

Yes. You should define and attach clear Partner/Affiliate Terms to your program. If partners violate these terms (fraud, self-referrals, incentivized traffic without approval, bots, misattribution), you have the right to withhold commission.

### Do I approve every piece of partner content?

No mandatory pre-approval is required—this would create friction and slow growth. Instead:

- You may perform random or targeted content reviews
- Raise complaints if content is misleading or non-compliant
- Communicate directly with partners via Affitor messaging
- Enforce violations after publication based on Partner Terms

This balances brand safety with scalability.

### What if a partner damages my brand?

You can request content removal with a prompt response window (typically 24-48 hours). If content violates guidelines and commissions are withheld, partners are naturally motivated to correct or remove content quickly.

## Troubleshooting / Integration

### "Test Click Tracking" shows "No Events Detected"

This means the dashboard hasn't received a pageview event from your site yet. Run through this checklist:

1. **Verify the script is loading** — Open DevTools → Network tab, filter by `affitor-tracker`. You should see a successful (200) request.
2. **Check your Program ID** — The `data-affitor-program-id` (script tag) or `programId` (npm SDK) must match the ID shown in your dashboard Settings.
3. **Visit with a `?ref=` parameter** — Pageview tracking activates when a visitor arrives via an affiliate link, e.g. `https://yoursite.com?ref=TEST123`.
4. **Disable ad blockers** — Some browser extensions (uBlock Origin, Privacy Badger) can block tracking scripts. Test in an incognito window with extensions disabled.
5. **HTTPS is required** — Affitor cookies require a secure context. `http://localhost` works for development, but production must use HTTPS.
6. **Check the browser console** — Enable debug mode (`data-affitor-debug="true"` or `debug` prop) and look for `[Affitor]` messages.

See the full [Pageview Tracker guide](/advertisers/tracking/pageview-tracker-click/) for setup details.

### Console shows no Affitor debug messages

If you don't see any `[Affitor]` log messages in the browser console:

- **Debug mode not enabled** — Add `data-affitor-debug="true"` to the script tag, or pass `debug` as a prop to `<AffitorProvider>`, or pass `{ debug: true }` to `loadAffitor()`.
- **Script not loaded** — Check DevTools → Network for the tracker script request. A 404 means the script URL is wrong. Make sure you're using the correct URL:
  - Production: `https://api.affitor.com/js/affitor-tracker.js`
  - UAT/Staging: `https://uat-api.affitor.com/js/affitor-tracker-uat.js`
- **Content Security Policy (CSP) blocking** — If your site has CSP headers, ensure they allow scripts from `api.affitor.com`.
- **Ad blocker interference** — Test in a clean browser profile without extensions.

### Cookies not being set (`affitor_click_id` missing)

If you check DevTools → Application → Cookies and don't see an `affitor_click_id` cookie:

- **No `?ref=` parameter** — The cookie is only set when a visitor arrives via `?ref=PARTNER_CODE`.
- **Not on HTTPS** — Cookies require a secure context in production. `http://localhost` is fine for development.
- **Browser privacy settings** — Some browsers (Safari with ITP, Firefox with ETP) or extensions may block first-party cookies. Check your browser's privacy settings.
- **CSP blocking** — Your Content Security Policy headers might be blocking cookie creation.

### "Test Lead Tracking" shows "No Events Detected"

This means the dashboard hasn't received a lead/signup event. Check the following:

1. **Pageview tracker must work first** — The `affitor_click_id` cookie (set by pageview tracking) is needed to link leads to affiliates. Complete and verify pageview tracking before testing lead tracking.
2. **`signup()` not called** — Ensure `signup()` is called *after* successful signup, not before or during form submission.
3. **`customerKey` is missing** — The `customerKey` (first argument) is **required**. Without it, lead events will not be processed for payment attribution.

   ```js
   // ✅ Correct
   await affitor?.signup('user_123', 'user@example.com');

   // ❌ Missing customerKey — will not attribute payments
   await affitor?.signup(undefined, 'user@example.com');
   ```

4. **Script tag: SDK not ready** — If using the script tag method, `window.affitor` may not be loaded yet. Use the queue fallback pattern:

   ```js
   if (window.affitor) {
     await window.affitor.signup(userId, email);
   } else {
     window.affitorQueue = window.affitorQueue || [];
     window.affitorQueue.push(['signup', userId, email]);
   }
   ```

5. **Check console output** — With debug mode on, you should see `[Affitor] signup called with: {customerKey: '...', email: '...'}` and `[Affitor] Lead event tracked`.

See the full [Lead Tracking guide](/advertisers/tracking/lead-tracking-signup/) for setup details.

### "Share Program" step is locked / shows red X

The Share Program step becomes available only after all previous setup steps are completed:

1. **Set Program Details** — Business details filled, approval mode configured, at least one commission tier created.
2. **Tracking & Integrations** — All three trackers (Pageview, Referrals, Payments) must show "Connected" status.

Complete any pending steps and the lock will automatically clear. Hover over the disabled step to see which prerequisite is missing.

### Integration works in development but not in production

If tracking works on `localhost` but breaks in production:

- **Wrong tracker script URL** — Development may use the UAT script (`affitor-tracker-uat.js`). Production must use `affitor-tracker.js`:
  ```html
  <!-- Production -->
  <script src="https://api.affitor.com/js/affitor-tracker.js" ...>

  <!-- NOT this in production -->
  <script src="https://uat-api.affitor.com/js/affitor-tracker-uat.js" ...>
  ```
- **CSP headers** — Production environments often have stricter Content Security Policy headers. Ensure `api.affitor.com` is allowed in `script-src` and `connect-src` directives.
- **HTTPS required** — Production must use HTTPS for cookies to work.
- **Debug mode left on** — Remove `data-affitor-debug="true"` or `debug` props before deploying. Debug mode still works in production but generates verbose console output.
- **Different domain** — Cookies are domain-scoped. Ensure your production domain matches what's configured in your Affitor program settings.

If issues persist after checking all the above, contact **support@affitor.com** with your Program ID, browser console screenshots, and steps to reproduce.
