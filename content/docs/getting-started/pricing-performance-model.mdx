Affitor uses performance-based pricing. You pay when partner-attributed revenue is recorded through your program.

## Core pricing model

- **Monthly subscription:** $0
- **Setup fee:** $0
- **Platform fee:** 3.5% of partner-generated revenue

## Example breakdown

A partner drives a $100 sale with a 30% commission:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Gross revenue | $100.00 |
| Stripe fee (example) | -$3.20 |
| Partner commission (30%) | -$30.00 |
| Affitor platform fee (3.5%) | -$3.50 |
| **You keep** | **$63.30** |

## How billing works in the current public model

Affitor's public docs currently describe a **Bill Flow** model:

1. you keep collecting customer payments in your own Stripe/payment stack
2. Affitor attributes validated affiliate conversions
3. Affitor bills through its invoice workflow for partner commission + platform fee obligations
4. partner payout operations continue through Affitor

:::note
Affitor is not the merchant of record in this Bill Flow model.
:::

## Cash flow and payout workflow

Commissions then move through Affitor's review / hold / payout process. Exact operational timing can vary by program settings and payout workflow, so treat your dashboard and commercial setup as the final operational reference.

[→ See full cash flow details](/advertisers/quickstart/commission-approval-cash-flow)

## $10K guarantee

Your first $10,000 in affiliate-driven revenue is fee-free according to current public pricing materials.

If you need confirmation on your exact commercial terms, confirm them with the Affitor team before launch.

## What's included

- partner tracking from click → signup → sale
- partner program management
- commission operations
- payout workflow support
- analytics and reporting
- partner messaging / coordination surfaces where available

## Best fit

Affitor works best when your team can do one of the following cleanly:

- send revenue from your backend using `POST /api/v1/track/sale`
- or attach the required metadata to Stripe Checkout in Bill Flow
